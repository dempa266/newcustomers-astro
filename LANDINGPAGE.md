# Digital Marketing Agency Service Page Layout

## Purpose

This document defines the **recommended layout structure for a digital marketing agency service landing page** (e.g., `/seo-agency`, `/ppc-agency`, `/digital-marketing-agency`).
The layout is optimized for **SEO visibility, trust building, and lead generation**.

The page should follow the psychological flow:

**Problem → Solution → Proof → Process → Offer → CTA**

---

# Page Structure

## 1. Hero Section

**Purpose:** Clearly communicate the service, target audience, and value proposition within the first few seconds.

**Content**

- Primary headline
- Supporting subheadline
- Primary call-to-action (CTA)
- Secondary CTA (optional)
- Key trust signals
- Visual or illustration

**Elements**

- Headline explaining the core service
- Short value proposition
- CTA (e.g., "Get Free Audit", "Book Strategy Call")
- Client logos or ratings
- Supporting visual or graphic

---

## 2. Client Logos / Social Proof

**Purpose:** Instantly build credibility and demonstrate industry trust.

**Content**

- Logos of recognizable clients
- Optional credibility indicators (years in business, clients served)

**Elements**

- Horizontal logo row
- Optional short heading (e.g., "Trusted by 200+ companies")

---

## 3. Problem / Pain Points

**Purpose:** Demonstrate understanding of the customer’s challenges.

**Content**

- Common problems businesses face related to the service
- Clear articulation of pain points

**Elements**

- Section headline
- Bullet points describing key challenges
- Short transition into your solution

---

## 4. Solution / Unique Approach

**Purpose:** Present the agency's core framework or methodology.

**Content**

- Overview of the agency's strategic approach
- Key differentiators

**Elements**

- Framework or methodology
- 3–4 pillars or components of the service
- Short explanation of each component

---

## 5. Results / Case Studies

**Purpose:** Provide evidence of success and measurable results.

**Content**

- Real examples of client outcomes
- Quantified performance metrics

**Elements**

- Case study cards
- Key results (traffic growth, leads, revenue)
- Summary of work performed
- Link to full case studies

---

## 6. Process / How It Works

**Purpose:** Explain the engagement workflow and reduce uncertainty.

**Content**

- Step-by-step overview of the agency process

**Elements**

- Timeline or step-based layout
- Typically 3–5 steps

Example structure:

1. Audit or discovery
2. Strategy development
3. Execution
4. Optimization and reporting

---

## 7. Services Breakdown

**Purpose:** Provide clarity about what is included in the offering.

**Content**

- List of specific services or capabilities related to the main service

**Elements**

- Service grid or list
- Short descriptions for each service component
